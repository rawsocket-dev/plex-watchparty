A · Grouped row + overflow kebab recommended

One row, three zones with visual grouping. Left: a compact status cluster (role LED · who's driving · sync) — Audience & BW collapse into a hover/tap popover. Center: title. Right: only the primary transport (Run/Pause) stays out; secondary tools (Volume, Fit, Fullscreen, Pass control, Admin, Exit) move into a ⋯ kebab menu. Cleanest; scales to narrow by dropping the status cluster into the kebab too.

B · Two-tier (status strip + control row)

Splits responsibilities onto two rows. A thin status strip up top (role · host · sync · audience · BW · title), and a control row below with buttons in labelled groups: [transport] · [view] · [host] · [exit]. Most legible hierarchy & room to grow, at the cost of vertical space (worse on short/landscape phones).

C · Minimal auto-reveal (media-player chrome)

A slim always-visible strip (title · play-state dot · who's driving · ⚙). Full controls slide in on hover/tap over the video, like Netflix/YouTube — shown here in the revealed state. Maximises screen for the movie; status/tools are one gesture away. Most "app-like", but hidden controls are less discoverable and fiddlier on a shared-TV remote.

— open questions these raise —

1. Phone target? If people watch on phones, A or C win (B eats vertical space).
2. Discoverability vs. minimalism — A/B keep transport always visible; C hides it behind a gesture.
3. What's allowed in the overflow ⋯ — Admin & Exit are rare; Volume/Fullscreen are common. Where's the line?
4. Viewer vs host vs admin — viewers see no transport/Pass/Admin; the layout should look intentional (not gap-riddled) in all three roles.
